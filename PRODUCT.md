# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Primary users are travelers in Pakistan, especially families, couples, honeymoon planners, and small private groups comparing northern tour routes before booking. They usually arrive while researching destinations like Skardu, Hunza, Swat, Kashmir, or Naran Kaghan and want a trustworthy agency that can turn interest into a clear itinerary.

## Product Purpose
This website exists to present Abid Tour & Travels as a credible, professional booking partner for Northern Pakistan tours, private travel packages, and vehicle rentals with driver. Success means visitors quickly understand the destinations, trust the agency, and contact the business with enough confidence to start a booking conversation.

## Brand Personality
Clean, premium, trustworthy. The tone should feel polished and calm, with enough warmth to support leisure travel without becoming decorative, flashy, or overly dark.

## Anti-references
Avoid flashy travel-site gimmicks, over-saturated gradients, noisy promo styling, heavy dark-mode aesthetics, and generic template cards with weak typography. The site should not feel like a discount booking portal, a nightclub-style luxury brand, or a playful influencer travel page.

## Design Principles
1. Lead with trust before excitement. Clear structure, readable typography, and grounded information should do more work than decorative effects.
2. Make scenic travel feel premium through restraint. Use imagery, spacing, and type hierarchy to create value instead of relying on loud color or crowded sections.
3. Keep booking paths obvious. Every important page should move naturally toward destination exploration or direct contact.
4. Let destinations feel distinct but consistent. Route pages and cards should feel part of one system while still giving each place its own sense of atmosphere.
5. Preserve clarity on mobile. Layouts must stay centered, balanced, and easy to scan on smaller screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility across the site. Keep heading structure semantic, preserve readable text sizing and spacing, and avoid motion or layered effects that reduce clarity. Prefer calm visual hierarchy over dense decorative layouts so the site remains usable for a broad travel audience.
